Introduction back to ToC

The EU Data Act (Regulation 2023/2854), applicable from September 12, 2025, establishes a unified legal framework for data access and sharing across the European Union. It governs three main contexts: - B2C (Business-to-Consumer): granting users access to data from their connected products. - B2B (Business-to-Business): enabling data sharing under user authorization and FRAND (fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory) conditions. - B2G (Business-to-Government): allowing access to private-sector data in cases of exceptional public need. Complying with these complex, interdependent legal obligations poses major challengesâ€”especially for SMEs, which often lack the resources for continuous legal oversight. Current compliance processes are manual, error-prone, and non-scalable, and existing tools focus mainly on GDPR or AI Act obligations, leaving the Data Act largely unsupported. To address this gap, this ontology introduces a machine-readable regulatory framework for the EU Data Act. By representing legal provisions in formal, semantic terms, it enables: - Automated deontic reasoning over obligations, permissions, and prohibitions. - Semantic querying to detect compliance or violations in specific contractual scenarios. - Interoperability with European data spaces through standards-based representations. This ontology integrates LegalRuleML, LKIF-Core, ODRL, and DPV to formalize Data Act rules and demonstrate automated reasoning for representative articles: - Article 4(1): user data access (B2C) - Article 8(6): trade secret exceptions (B2B) - Article 19(2)(a): prohibition of competitive use (B2G) Through SPARQL-based reasoning examples, this project showcases how formal ontologies can make Data Act compliance verifiable, interoperable, and automatable.
